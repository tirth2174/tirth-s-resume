Accept Stutter

By Tirth Patel

Accept Stutter is not just a podcast, it’s a platform focused on showcasing the stories of people who stutter; it’s a platform for scientists and experts doing groundbreaking research on stuttering; it’s a platform for individuals who are passionate about helping people who stutter; Above all that, this is a place for everyone. So, whether you stutter or not, come to the accept stutter podcast to share your story, and let’s together talk about stuttering. I am your host, Tirth Patel, a student at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor, and I am a person who stutters.

Educating, advocating, and empowering for stuttering with Ezra (Jaymie) Horak, the founder of Stutterology podcast

"Journey of accepting your stutter takes time. You should stay encouraged to know that even if you have days where you're feeling heavy and sad and wishing things were easier. It doesn't mean that you have lost progress because you're already miles ahead than you were before and you're already doing okay," says Ezra (Jaymie) Horak, the founder of Stutterology podcast. Ezra has taken several initiatives in the stuttering community, and hence, she is very well known in this community. Listen to this podcast to explore Ezra's journey with stuttering. Reach out to @stutterology on Instagram to learn more about the Stutterology podcast. Subscribe to Accept Stutter on YouTube and every podcasting platform. Follow us on Instagram @accept_stutter. While listening to this episode, if there was any part you could not understand, feel free to DM me on Instagram, comment on YouTube, or email me your questions at istutter9999@gmail.com. If you are interested in coming to this podcast as a guest, please let me know on Instagram or through email (istutter9999@gmail.com). Until then, stay tuned for future episodes.
